Version 1.0 — Last Updated: December 2025


1. Purpose of This Code

This Code of Conduct establishes the ethical, professional, and behavioral standards expected from all members, partners, volunteers, contributors, leaders, and community participants of CeloHT.
It is designed to:

Promote a safe, inclusive, and respectful environment

Ensure integrity, transparency, and accountability

Protect the reputation and mission of CeloHT

Align with international Web3 and community governance norms


All members agree to comply with this Code as a condition of participation.



2. Core Values of CeloHT

CeloHT operates based on the following international principles:

2.1 Integrity

Act honestly and transparently in all activities.

2.2 Respect

Treat all individuals with dignity, regardless of background or status.

2.3 Inclusiveness

Provide equal opportunities and promote diversity.

2.4 Responsibility

Protect community resources, data, and reputation.

2.5 Environmental Respect

Support Celo’s carbon-negative mission and ecological initiatives.



3. Expected Behavior

All members must:

3.1 Respect Others

Engage with kindness and professionalism

Avoid offensive language, discrimination, or insults

Value cultural differences, especially within the Haitian diaspora


3.2 Promote a Safe Environment

Encourage constructive dialogue

Help resolve conflicts peacefully

Support newcomers and educate the community


3.3 Act with Transparency

Share accurate information

Avoid rumors, manipulation, or misleading statements

Declare factual errors immediately when identified


3.4 Follow Leadership Guidance

Comply with decisions from CeloHT’s coordinators, moderators, and governance bodies.



4. Unacceptable Behavior

The following actions are strictly prohibited:

4.1 Harassment or Abuse

Including:

Verbal or written threats

Hate speech

Sexual harassment

Bullying or intimidation

Persistent unwanted communication


4.2 Discrimination

Based on nationality, gender, religion, ethnicity, disability, sexual orientation, social class, or any protected characteristic.

4.3 Fraud or Misrepresentation

Pretending to represent CeloHT without authorization

Creating fake partnerships or misleading the public

Fabricating statistics or financial claims


4.4 Spamming or Scamming

Including crypto-scams, phishing, Ponzi schemes, and any deceptive financial activity.

4.5 Misuse of Resources

Using CeloHT tools, platforms, logo, documentation, or funds for personal gain

Unauthorized announcements or fundraising



5. Conflict of Interest Policy

All members must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with CeloHT’s mission.

5.1 Required Actions

Members must:

Decline decisions that could benefit them personally

Declare conflicts immediately to the leadership

Avoid using inside information for private advantage


Examples:

Promoting your personal project under the CeloHT brand

Requesting donations for personal use

Accepting gifts or incentives that may influence decisions




6. Anti-Corruption Policy (Zero Tolerance)

CeloHT strictly rejects:

Bribery

Undocumented financial transactions

Gift exchanges intended to gain influence

Misuse or diversion of funds


All financial activities must be transparent, documented, and auditable.




7. Cybersecurity & Data Protection

Because CeloHT operates in the Web3 ecosystem, members must comply with strict digital safety rules:

7.1 Member Responsibilities

Protect personal wallets and private keys

Never request or share recovery phrases

Report phishing or suspicious links immediately

Avoid impersonation or hacking attempts


7.2 Community Protection

CeloHT communications must follow secure channels only.




8. Inclusivity Statement

CeloHT is committed to building a diverse and welcoming environment.

We support:

Equal access to information

Respect for all identities

Participation from beginners, professionals, and underrepresented groups


No member may exclude, silence, or undermine others based on personal prejudice.



9. Protection of Minors

If minors participate in educational events:

No private messaging without parental consent

No inappropriate discussions

All interactions must remain public and supervised

Leaders must ensure minors are safe at all times




10. Environmental Responsibility

In alignment with Celo’s carbon-negative mission, CeloHT commits to:

Support ecological education

Promote green technologies

Reduce environmental impact during events

Encourage community actions that protect the planet




11. Use of Brand, Platforms & Resources

The CeloHT name, logo, documentation, communication channels, and visual identity must NOT be used for personal or unauthorized activities.

Prohibited uses include:

Personal business promotion

Fake partnerships

Paid advertisements without approval

Unauthorized fundraising


All official materials must receive approval from the CeloHT leadership.




12. Reporting Misconduct

Members can confidentially report:

Violations of the Code

Harassment

Safety concerns

Fraud

Misuse of funds or authority


Reports may be sent to the CeloHT leadership team privately.



13. Investigation Process

Upon receiving a complaint:

1. The leadership acknowledges the report


2. A neutral committee reviews evidence


3. Both sides may be heard


4. A decision is made based on fairness and community safety


5. Sanctions are applied if needed




14. Sanctions

Depending on severity, sanctions may include:

Warning

Temporary removal from groups

Permanent ban

Revocation of privileges

Official public notice

Removal from leadership roles


All sanctions aim to protect the community and uphold standards.



15. Right to Appeal

Every member has the right to request a second review within 7 days of sanction notification.

Appeals must provide:

New evidence

Context

Explanation of disagreement


The appeal will be reviewed by a separate committee to avoid bias.



16. Leadership Ethical Standards

Leaders and moderators must:

Act with fairness and neutrality

Prevent abuse of power

Maintain transparency in decisions

Avoid favoritism

Protect the integrity of the community


Leadership is a responsibility, not a privilege.



17. Commitment to Celo’s Vision

CeloHT supports the greater Celo ecosystem by promoting:

Financial inclusion

Environmental sustainability

Blockchain innovation

Honest communication

Positive growth in the Haitian community



18. Acceptance of This Code

Participation in any CeloHT activity means you agree to follow this Code of Conduct.

Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action as described above.
