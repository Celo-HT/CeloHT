# Governance

## Philosophy

CeloHT is meant to be **governed by its community**, not run top-down. The
point of this governance model is to keep decisions transparent, accountable,
and aligned with the three founding pillars: Education, Agent Network, and
Reforestation.

## Structure

### 1. Founder / Foundation Director

Owns the overall strategic vision, represents CeloHT externally (partnerships,
investors, the Celo Foundation), and has the final say when the governance
process reaches a genuine deadlock.

### 2. Maintainer Council

A group of maintainers responsible for technical review, Pull Request
approval, and the day-to-day running of this repository. See
[`docs/maintainers.md`](docs/maintainers.md) for the current list and
[`CODEOWNERS`](CODEOWNERS) for who owns what.

### 3. Community Contributors

Anyone submitting Issues, Pull Requests, or joining Discussions. Contributors
who show consistent, high-quality involvement may be invited onto the
Maintainer Council.

## How Decisions Get Made

- **Minor changes** (typo fixes, documentation improvements): approved by
  any maintainer after a standard review.
- **Major changes** (new structure, policy shifts, brand changes): require
  consensus from the Maintainer Council, discussed publicly before being
  applied.
- **Governance changes** (edits to this document itself): require a minimum
  14-day public comment period and majority approval from the Maintainer
  Council.

## Transparency

Every major decision is documented publicly in
[GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/celo-ht/celoht/discussions) and
reflected in [`CHANGELOG.md`](CHANGELOG.md) and
[`docs/roadmap.md`](docs/roadmap.md).

## Resolving Disagreements

Governance disagreements are worked out first through open discussion within
the Maintainer Council. If no consensus emerges, the Foundation Director
makes the final call, along with a public explanation of the reasoning.

## Where This Is Headed

This governance model is designed to evolve as the community grows —
potentially toward something more decentralized, like an elected council or
a lightweight DAO structure. See [`docs/roadmap.md`](docs/roadmap.md) for the
longer-term plan.
