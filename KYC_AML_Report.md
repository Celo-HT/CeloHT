# KYC/AML REPORT – CELOHT

## 1. Entity Identification
**Name:** CeloHT  
**Nature:** Educational and agent-facilitated crypto access initiative  
**Operational Model:** Agent-assisted P2P facilitation (non-custodial)  
**Blockchain Ecosystem:** Celo (cUSD via Valora or compatible wallets)

## 2. Regulatory Classification (Corrected)
CeloHT acknowledges that its **agent-facilitated P2P model**, involving:
- human agents,
- transaction facilitation,
- service fees,

**may qualify as a Money Services Business (MSB) or equivalent intermediary** depending on jurisdiction.

CeloHT therefore operates under a **risk-aware MSB-aligned compliance framework**, even where formal registration is pending or jurisdiction-dependent.

## 3. Jurisdictional Scope
CeloHT operates in a **multi-jurisdictional environment**, primarily in emerging markets.

Compliance framework references:
- FATF Recommendations
- Risk-Based Approach (RBA)
- Local AML/CFT laws where agents operate

Final regulatory classification is determined **per country of operation**.

## 4. Purpose of KYC/AML Policy
- Prevent money laundering and terrorist financing
- Reduce fraud and abuse
- Protect users, agents, and ecosystem partners
- Enable future regulatory engagement and banking access

## 5. Risk Assessment
### 5.1 Inherent Risks
- Cash ↔ crypto agent interaction
- P2P facilitation
- Cross-border value transfer
- Stablecoin usage

### 5.2 Risk Level
**Overall Risk Level:** Medium–High  
**Mitigation Strategy:**  
Strict agent KYC, tiered user due diligence, transaction thresholds, and behavioral monitoring.

## 6. KYC Scope 
### 6.1 Agents (Mandatory Full KYC)
Required:
- Full legal name
- Government-issued ID
- Date of birth
- Nationality
- Address or location proof
- Contact details
- Criminal background self-declaration
- Role and transaction limits

Agents **cannot operate without completed KYC**.

### 6.2 End Users (Tiered KYC)
CeloHT applies **risk-based, tiered user identification**:

- **Low volume:** basic identity (name + phone)
- **Medium volume:** ID verification via agent
- **High volume / repeated use:** enhanced due diligence

No anonymous usage is permitted via agents.

## 7. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
- Transaction limits per user tier
- Geographic risk assessment
- Purpose-of-transaction checks
- Ongoing agent supervision

## 8. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)
Triggered when:
- High-frequency or high-value transactions
- Cross-border concentration
- Structuring behavior
- Red flags reported by agents

EDD Measures:
- Additional ID
- Source of funds explanation
- Temporary suspension pending review

## 9. Transaction Visibility & Monitoring (Corrected)
Although CeloHT does not custody funds, it **does have indirect transaction visibility** through:

- Agent transaction logs
- User declarations
- Wallet addresses involved
- Volume and frequency tracking

Monitoring includes:
- Threshold alerts
- Pattern detection
- Manual compliance review

## 10. Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)
### 10.1 Internal
Agents must immediately report:
- Suspected fraud
- Identity mismatch
- Coercion or scam indicators
- Structuring attempts

### 10.2 External
When required by applicable law:
- Reports are filed with the competent authority
- Cooperation is limited to legal obligations
- Data disclosure is proportional and documented

## 11. Sanctions Screening
- Agents screened against international sanctions lists
- Users screened when risk thresholds are crossed
- Immediate suspension upon positive match

## 12. Record Keeping 
- KYC and transaction records retained **minimum 5 years**
- Secure, access-controlled storage
- Records available for lawful audits

## 13. Data Protection
- Data minimization
- Encryption at rest and in transit
- No commercial sale of personal data
- Access limited to compliance roles

## 14. Training and Compliance Culture
- Mandatory AML/KYC training for agents
- Regular updates on fraud typologies
- Written code of conduct

## 15. Relationship With Valora and Celo
- Valora provides wallet infrastructure
- CeloHT remains operationally independent
- Compliance responsibility is **not delegated**; reliance is supplementary only

## 16. Enforcement
Violations result in:
- Immediate suspension
- Role termination
- Reporting to authorities if required

## 17. Governance
- Compliance Officer designated
- Periodic internal audits
- Policy updates based on risk evolution

## 18. Limitations
CeloHT cannot:
- Freeze blockchain funds
- Reverse transactions
- Override wallet-level controls

These limitations are disclosed to users and agents.

## 19. Review Cycle
- Annual review
- Immediate revision upon legal or operational change

## 20. Declaration
CeloHT commits to a **realistic, MSB-aware, risk-based AML/KYC framework**, aligned with international standards and adapted to emerging-market realities.
