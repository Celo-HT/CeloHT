# CeloHT is A Web3 NGO for Financial Inclusion and Social Impact

## 1. Introduction
**CeloHT** is a **Non-Governmental Organization (NGO)** leveraging Web3 technology to build accessible, responsible, and utility-driven digital infrastructure for underserved communities.

The initiative starts in Haiti and is designed to expand across the Caribbean and other regions where access to financial education and modern digital payment tools remains limited.

CeloHT is **not** a bank, **not** an investment platform.



## 2. Mission
CeloHT’s mission is to:
- Provide practical financial education
- Enable access to non-custodial digital payments
- Deliver measurable and transparent environmental impact

All activities are conducted without speculation, custody of user funds, or financial intermediation.



## 3. The Three Core Pillars of CeloHT

### 3.1 Financial Education
Financial education is at the core of CeloHT’s mission.

This includes:
- Digital financial literacy
- Understanding stablecoins and real-world use cases
- Wallet usage and self-custody principles

CeloHT does **not** provide personalized investment advice and does **not** manage user funds.


### 3.2 Non-Custodial Digital Payments  
**(Celo cUSD & Valora App)**

CeloHT promotes the use of existing tools within the **Celo blockchain ecosystem**, including:

- **cUSD**: a stablecoin pegged to the US dollar
- **Valora App**: a simple, mobile-first, non-custodial digital wallet

Key principles:
- Users retain full control of their private keys
- CeloHT does not hold deposits
- CeloHT does not execute transactions on behalf of users
- No financial intermediation

CeloHT acts as an **education and infrastructure enabler**



### 3.3 Measurable Environmental Impact
Environmental action is a strategic pillar of CeloHT.

This includes:
- Reforestation initiatives
- CO₂ impact mitigation
- Transparent and verifiable impact reporting

CeloHT demonstrates that blockchain technology can support **sustainable development**, not just financial use cases.
