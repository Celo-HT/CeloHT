# CeloHT Impact Framework

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how CeloHT measures, monitors, and reports the impact of its initiatives.  

The impact framework ensures alignment with CeloHT’s mission:
- Financial education
- Measurable reforestation and environmental impact
- Accessible digital payments using Celo, cUSD, and the Valora app

All initiatives are evaluated to maximize social, educational, and environmental benefits.

---

## 2. Impact Principles

- **Measurability**: All outcomes must be quantifiable where possible.
- **Transparency**: Impact metrics are publicly documented and auditable.
- **Mission Alignment**: Only initiatives aligned with the three core pillars are tracked.
- **Sustainability**: Projects are designed for long-term positive outcomes.
- **Community Participation**: Stakeholders can contribute to evaluation and reporting.

---

## 3. Core Impact Areas

### 3.1 Financial Education
- **Objectives**: Increase digital and financial literacy among underserved communities.
- **Metrics**:
  - Number of participants in workshops and programs
  - Knowledge improvement via pre- and post-assessments
  - Adoption of Celo, cUSD, and Valora
- **Data Sources**: Workshop attendance logs, surveys, Valora usage statistics.

---

### 3.2 Reforestation & Environmental Impact
- **Objectives**: Implement measurable tree planting and carbon offset initiatives.
- **Metrics**:
  - Number of trees planted
  - Survival rate of trees after 6–12 months
  - CO₂ offset calculated using standard methodologies
- **Data Sources**: Satellite imagery, field verification, partner reports.

---

### 3.3 Digital Payments & Financial Inclusion
- **Objectives**: Enable access to digital payments via Celo ecosystem.
- **Metrics**:
  - Number of users onboarding with Valora
  - Volume and frequency of cUSD transactions
  - Number of underserved individuals gaining financial access
- **Data Sources**: On-chain transaction data, Valora app analytics.

---

## 4. Reporting Cadence

- **Quarterly Reports**: Public summary of initiatives, metrics, and outcomes.
- **Annual Impact Report**: Detailed report including KPIs, lessons learned, and improvements.
- **DAO Transition Phase**: Community may review and approve impact reporting methodology.

---

## 5. Data Governance

- All data collection complies with privacy and local regulations.
- Only aggregate, anonymized data is publicly reported.
- Records are stored securely and linked to treasury and proposals when relevant.

---

## 6. Review & Verification

- Core Team collects and validates data.
- Advisory Circle provides independent review.
- Selected community members may audit and provide feedback.
- Multisig approval is required if impact reporting affects treasury allocation.

---

## 7. Impact-Driven Funding

- Treasury allocations are guided by measurable impact outcomes.
- High-impact initiatives receive priority funding.
- All spending decisions are documented and traceable via multisig and proposal frameworks.

---

## 8. Continuous Improvement

- KPIs and methodologies are periodically updated.
- Lessons learned from each initiative inform future programs.
- Community feedback is integrated into impact assessment.

---

## 9. DAO Compatibility

- Impact reporting aligns with future DAO governance.
- Data and metrics may be used in on-chain voting or funding allocation decisions.
- Framework is designed to evolve without compromising mission integrity.

---

## 10. Legal Disclaimer

Impact data is intended for transparency, reporting, and accountability.  
Participation or support does not imply ownership, equity, or financial returns.

---

## 11. Effective Date

This impact framework is effective upon publication and remains valid until amended.
