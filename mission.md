# Mission

## Our Mission

**Expand financial inclusion through education, a decentralized network of
community agents, and environmental reforestation built on the Celo
ecosystem as our technical foundation.**

## How We Actually Do This

### Education comes first, always

Nobody should have to use a financial tool they don't understand. That's why
every CeloHT initiative starts with education in Haitian Creole, the
language people actually think in before we ever introduce a specific tool
like Valora or cUSD.

### Agents as a human bridge

Apps alone don't solve the trust problem. That's why we build a network of
real people in these communities who handle cash-in and cash-out,
facilitate transfers, and support people who aren't yet comfortable
navigating the technology on their own.

### Reforestation as long-term infrastructure

We treat the environment the same way we treat financial access: as
infrastructure the community genuinely needs, not a side project. That means
coordinated tree-planting tied directly into the agent network and rewarded
through cUSD.

## Who We're Building For

Rural and semi-rural communities in Haiti first, and other Caribbean
communities facing a similar reality: limited traditional banking access,
growing mobile phone use, and a real need for financial education that's
adapted both culturally and linguistically.
