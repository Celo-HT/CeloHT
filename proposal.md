# CeloHT Governance Proposal Framework

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how proposals are created, reviewed, and decided within CeloHT governance.

The proposal process ensures that decisions:
- Align with CeloHT’s mission
- Protect the three core pillars
- Support transparency and accountability
- Prepare CeloHT for progressive DAO governance

---

## 2. Scope of Proposals

Proposals may relate to:

- Financial education programs
- Reforestation and environmental impact initiatives
- Use of Celo, cUSD, and Valora app
- Treasury and multisig operations
- Governance updates
- Strategic partnerships
- DAO transition planning

Speculative or mission-misaligned proposals are rejected.

---

## 3. Who Can Submit a Proposal

During the current phase:
- Core Team members
- Approved contributors
- Community members (via discussion-first process)

All proposals must be documented publicly.

---

## 4. Proposal Categories

### 4.1 Operational Proposal
Small-scale actions required for day-to-day execution.

Approval: Core Team

---

### 4.2 Strategic Proposal
Medium or long-term decisions impacting direction, funding, or partnerships.

Approval:
- Core Team
- Advisory Circle review
- Community feedback period

---

### 4.3 Mission-Critical Proposal
Decisions that affect:
- Core pillars
- Treasury structure
- Governance model
- DAO transition

Approval:
- Public documentation
- Advisory Circle review
- Community consultation
- Multisig execution (if funds involved)

---

## 5. Proposal Template

Each proposal must include:

1. **Title**
2. **Author**
3. **Date**
4. **Category**
5. **Summary**
6. **Problem Statement**
7. **Proposed Solution**
8. **Alignment with CeloHT Pillars**
9. **Use of Celo / cUSD / Valora**
10. **Budget (if applicable)**
11. **Risks and Mitigation**
12. **Execution Plan**
13. **Expected Impact**
14. **Review Status**

Incomplete proposals are not reviewed.

---

## 6. Review Process

1. Proposal submitted and published
2. Initial validation (mission alignment)
3. Feedback and discussion period
4. Internal or advisory review
5. Final decision documented
6. Execution or rejection recorded publicly

---

## 7. Treasury-Related Proposals

Any proposal involving funds must:
- Specify amount and currency (preferably cUSD)
- Identify multisig execution requirement
- Align with treasury and multisig rules

No funds are moved without multisig approval.

---

## 8. Community Participation

Community members may:
- Comment on proposals
- Suggest improvements
- Raise concerns

Community feedback is advisory during the current phase and may become binding in future DAO phases.

---

## 9. Transparency and Record Keeping

All proposals and decisions:
- Are stored publicly (GitHub or governance platform)
- Remain accessible for audit and review
- Cannot be deleted once approved or rejected

---

## 10. Rejection Criteria

A proposal is rejected if it:
- Conflicts with CeloHT’s mission
- Introduces speculation-driven objectives
- Lacks clarity or accountability
- Violates legal or ethical standards

---

## 11. DAO Transition Compatibility

This proposal framework is designed to evolve into:
- On-chain proposal submission
- Tokenless or token-based voting
- Automated execution via smart contracts

Any transition will be documented publicly.

---

## 12. Disclaimer

Submitting or supporting a proposal does not grant ownership, profit rights, or financial guarantees.

---

## 13. Effective Date

This proposal framework is effective upon publication and remains valid until amended.
