# CeloHT Treasury Framework

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how CeloHT manages, allocates, and protects its financial resources.

The treasury exists solely to support CeloHTâ€™s mission:
- Financial education
- Measurable reforestation and environmental impact
- Accessible digital payments using Celo, cUSD, and the Valora app

The treasury is not designed for speculation or profit maximization.

---

## 2. Treasury Principles

CeloHT treasury management follows these principles:

- **Mission Alignment**: Every fund movement must support at least one core pillar.
- **Transparency**: Treasury addresses and transactions are publicly verifiable.
- **Security**: Funds are protected using multisig infrastructure.
- **Accountability**: Clear rules govern who can propose, approve, and execute spending.
- **Sustainability**: Treasury decisions prioritize long-term impact.

---

## 3. Treasury Assets

The treasury may hold:

- **cUSD**  
  Primary currency for operations, education programs, community incentives, and reforestation activities.

- **CELO**  
  Used for network fees, ecosystem participation, governance-related actions, and long-term alignment with the Celo blockchain.

No speculative assets are held by default.

---

## 4. Treasury Infrastructure

- **Blockchain**: Celo
- **Wallet Type**: Multisignature smart contract wallet
- **Execution**: Governed by the multisig framework
- **User Interface**: Compatible with Valora app where applicable

Treasury addresses are disclosed publicly.

---

## 5. Budget Categories

Treasury funds may be allocated to:

### 5.1 Education
- Financial literacy programs
- Web3 onboarding
- Community workshops and materials

### 5.2 Environmental Impact
- Tree planting initiatives
- Monitoring and verification of reforestation
- Environmental partnerships

### 5.3 Digital Payments & Inclusion
- cUSD distribution programs
- Payment infrastructure support
- User onboarding via Valora

### 5.4 Operations
- Technical infrastructure
- Communication and outreach
- Minimal administrative costs

---

## 6. Spending Rules

- All spending must be proposed and documented
- Treasury-related proposals follow `proposal.md`
- Any fund movement requires multisig approval
- Emergency spending is documented retroactively

No single individual may unilaterally move funds.

---

## 7. Approval Flow

1. Treasury proposal submitted
2. Purpose, amount, and category defined
3. Review for mission alignment
4. Multisig approval collected
5. Transaction executed on-chain
6. Record archived publicly

---

## 8. Transparency and Reporting

CeloHT commits to:
- Public treasury addresses
- On-chain traceability of all transactions
- Periodic treasury summaries
- Clear separation between operational and program funds

---

## 9. Risk Management

Identified risks include:
- Misuse of funds
- Operational errors
- Security breaches
- Mission drift

Mitigation measures:
- Multisig controls
- Proposal requirements
- Public visibility
- Advisory oversight when needed

---

## 10. Treasury Evolution (DAO-Ready)

As CeloHT transitions toward DAO governance:
- Treasury decisions may incorporate community voting
- On-chain execution may be automated
- Multisig remains active until DAO execution is secure

Treasury rules evolve without compromising mission protection.

---

## 11. Legal Disclaimer

Treasury participation does not grant ownership, equity, or guaranteed financial return.

Funds are used exclusively for mission-driven activities.

---

## 12. Effective Date

This treasury framework is effective upon publication and remains valid until amended.
